# Non-Stationary Stochastic Risk Engine for Nonferrous Metal Smelting
## Overview
The Non-Stationary Stochastic Risk Engine is designed to simulate and analyze the risks associated with nonferrous metal smelting and refining. The engine uses a combination of historical data and stochastic models to forecast future prices and production levels.
## Components
* **Database**: stores historical production and price data for nonferrous metals
* **Simulation Engine**: runs stochastic simulations to forecast future prices and production levels
* **Risk Analysis**: calculates risk metrics such as value-at-risk (VaR) and expected shortfall (ES)
## Workflow
1. Data ingestion: load historical production and price data into the database
2. Simulation: run stochastic simulations to forecast future prices and production levels
3. Risk analysis: calculate risk metrics using the simulated data
4. Reporting: generate reports and visualizations to communicate the results
